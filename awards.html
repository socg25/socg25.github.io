SoCG Test of Time Award 2025

The SoCG Test of Time Award 2025 goes to:

  • Raimund Seidel: Linear Programming and Convex Hulls Made Easy. SoCG 1990: 211-215 (proceedings version, journal version).
  • David Cohen-Steiner, Herbert Edelsbrunner, John Harer: Stability of persistence diagrams. SoCG 2005: 263-271 (proceedings version, journal version).

The awards will be presented in a special session of CG Week 2025.

  • Call for Nominations (already closed)

    The SoCG Test of Time Award, instated five years ago, recognizes papers published in the Proceedings of the Annual Symposium on Computational Geometry (SoCG). An award will be given to a paper published at SoCG no later than 2005. More than one paper may be selected for the award.

  • Nomination Procedure

    Anyone in the computational geometry community may nominate a paper by submitting the nomination via e-mail to socgtot@gmail.com with a subject line of SoCG Test of Time Award. The nomination should contain a brief explanation of the impact of the nominated paper(s). The deadline for submitting the nomination is March 15, 2025.

  • Selection

    The recipients are selected by a committee appointed by the SoCG steering committee. For 2025 it consists of Pankaj K. Agarwal (Duke University), Mark de Berg (TU Eindhoven), Siu-Wing Cheng (HKUST), and Yusu Wang (UCSD).

    In selecting the Test of Time Award, the committee will pay particular attention to long term impact. This impact can come in many forms. Some possibilities are: (i) to open up a new area of research; (ii) to introduce new techniques; (iii) to solve a problem of lasting importance. The selection will be announced by April 07, 2025.

  • About the Award

    This is the sixth SoCG Test of Time Award. The recipients of the previous awards can be found at https://computational-geometry.org/Awards/SoCG_Test_of_Time.html. The 2025 award recipients will be presented at CG Week 2025, to be held in Kanazawa, Japan, June 23–27, 2025.
